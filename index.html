Course Description

Building on the foundation established in EDL 830 (Doctoral Inquiry Seminar), this independent study course guides you through the development and defense of your dissertation proposal. Working one-on-one with your professor, you will complete drafts of Chapters 1 (Introduction), 2 (Literature Review), and 3 (Methodology), culminating in a formal proposal defense.


Prerequisite: EDL 830 - Doctoral Inquiry Seminar (Fall 2025)


Format: Independent study with regular professor meetings. Each weekly module includes interactive exercises, quizzes, and self-assessments to guide your learning.

Course Phases

Phase 1: Foundations & Chapter 2

Weeks 1-4

Review EDL 830 work, advanced literature review, synthesis skills, and theoretical/conceptual framework development.

Phase 2: Chapter 1 Development

Weeks 5-7

Problem statement, research questions, purpose, significance, and delimitations.

Phase 3: Chapter 3 Development

Weeks 8-12

Research design, sampling, data collection, data analysis, and ethical considerations.

Phase 4: Integration & Defense

Weeks 13-16

Chapter integration, revision, proposal defense, and transition to research.

Tips for Success

  • Set a consistent schedule - Block dedicated writing time each week
  • Complete interactive exercises - Quizzes and reflections reinforce learning
  • Meet regularly with your professor - Weekly or bi-weekly check-ins keep you on track
  • Submit work before meetings - Give your professor time to provide feedback
  • Track your progress - Use the built-in progress tracking in each module
