Phase 2: Chapter 1 Development

Overview

Your problem statement is the foundation of Chapter 1 and drives your entire dissertation. This week you'll refine your research problem, build compelling arguments for its significance, and craft a scholarly problem statement.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this week, you will be able to:

1
Distinguish between practical and research problems
2
Develop compelling significance arguments
3
Craft a focused problem statement with proper scope
4
Support your problem with empirical evidence
5
Connect problem statement to research questions

Independent Work Tasks

Complete these tasks and check them off as you go:

Refine Your Problem

Transform your practical concern into a scholarly research problem with clear boundaries.

Build Significance Arguments

Develop 3-4 compelling arguments for why this problem matters.

Gather Supporting Evidence

Collect empirical data and citations demonstrating the problem's importance.

Draft Problem Statement

Write a complete problem statement section (2-3 pages).
